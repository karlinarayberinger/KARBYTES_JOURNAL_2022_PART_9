KARBYTES_JOURNAL_2022_ENTRY_ANTICIPATING_LIFESTYLE_UPGRADE

After brushing my teeth, flossing my teeth, brushing my hair, and doing pushups in the dirt lot south of the Dublin BART station parking garage, I started walking towards Persimmons shopping center. On my way, I encountered a dope dealer on a bike who said he was from San Jose and we chatted for a bit about random shit. Then I kept going and attempted to purchase shampoo, hair conditioner, laundry detergent, and an energy drink from Whole Foods Market only to see that the transaction was declined. I was able to purchase the energy drink but I left the other items behind. Then I used the Whole Foods Wi-Fi to check my Patelco checking account to see how much money is in my account and saw that there was only $35. CafePress did not charge me approximately $100 for the recent custom shirt order I placed until last night or this morning. That’s why I had less money than I expected while ringing up store items.

I was buying the hair products and laundry detergent because I was suddenly planning on moving back to my mom’s house on the west side of Castro Valley. I think that I might do well to move there because (a) the air quality is better than it is here in the flats where the car exhaust piles up and (b) my mom’s house is next to a horse pasture which is up in the hills, the outside of her house has electrical outlets, (c) I could use her house to shower and do laundry, (d) her house has better Wi-Fi than my dad’s house does, and (e) her house is conveniently located closer to Casto Valley BART (and BayFair BART) and is in a less claustrophobic part of the world than my dad’s house is.

In short, I am getting ready to live at the horse pastures near my mom’s house because that seems to be a cleaner and more convenient place than all the other locales I have been attempting to live at. It is simply not feasible for me to hold down a job and have to travel by foot from Lake Chabot campgrounds to the city. It is feasible for me to camp at “Horsey Hill” and walk to Castro Valley BART station because that trip is all down hill and only takes about an hour. Though I could use the car which is parked at my mom’s house, I still plan to not use it because I do not want to rely on fossil fuels for my commute. Whether or not I get a job any time soon, I still plan to take regular trips to the Whole Foods Market in Dublin because it is a nice place and I like the products there. (It’s not much pricier than Safeway).

Anyway, I am feeling better than I have in months. I think that spending too much time on my dad’s side of Castro Valley has made me depressed because it feels cramped yet isolating and far removed from the open fields which make “Horsey Hill” less boxed in than the narrow trails and roads on the east side of Castro Valley. No matter where I go within the Bay Area, annoying voices follow me around and try to make me feel that I am in a “no win” situation.

I thought that I might do better at the horse pasture and at my mom’s house because that area is where I spent my early twenties learning C++ and getting into technology and futurism for the first time. (Spencer and I used to live in my old bedroom at my mom’s house some of the time and most of the time at his bedroom at his dad’s house in San Lorenzo. Perhaps I am indulging my nostalgia today and letting it fill my creative process with good vibes).

The voices are telling me that I am being a female if I try using the area near my mom’s house (which is an insult). That side is very close to John George Psychiatric Pavilian and the juvenile hall (which are rather depressing institutions). I am trying to get my mind maps updated such that they are not so cede and filled with ugly memories and ugly associations. That’s part of the reason why I plan on commuting to Whole Foods in Dublin almost every day until I get a job. The voices seem to bully me unless I go to Dublin (but they bully me here to). Those voices are insinuating that there is nothing I can do but sleep my life away while being harassed by the police. I think they want me to feel that I am being prevented from having what I think is a minimally decent standard of living (which includes making software development a central staple of my daily life and not just something I dabble in once in a while). When I hear other people talking (including right here in Dublin), I here people taunting me that I am not allowed to think about myself and that I am supposed to feel bullied and deprived of happiness.

People generally seem opposed to me working on my primary website. They seem to think it is criminally self indulgent and criminally irrelevant to what the masses are interested in. Perhaps I am spending too much time in the vicinity of political conservatives. Well, I will back up this note now and head to the west side of Castro Valley. I will make another journal entry update after I arrive. (Perhaps the voices are bullying me because they do not want me to have that lifestyle upgrade. A lot of the people in this part of Dublin seem very uptight and politically conservative. I am getting sick of their misery mongering ways. That female voice who whines incessantly for hours is particularly annoying. She sounds like a colicky baby throwing a tantrum while appearing to be a full grown adult wearing a tiara).

Breaking the fourth wall: I do not think the voices are causing me more harm than good. I think the voices are helping me avoid wasting too much time. They have nagged me into getting my trial and error stuff done more quickly than I probably would have without them. I had to test various living situations to find out which ones are likely going to allow me to attain and to maintain the kind of lifestyle I want. What I want is to be able to throw myself into being a nerd full time without having to keep slamming the breaks. I could potentially land a job in software development if I have a good enough portfolio and get in enough programming practice. I could do that even while having a part time retail job. In fact, I think that having a part-time retail job and being a full time computer science and physics student is just what I really want. I could do that for years and then, after several years of that, I will have the confidence and competence I need to get a full time job in information technology. How’s that for a lifestyle upgrade?
