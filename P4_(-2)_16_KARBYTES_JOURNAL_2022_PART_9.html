KARBYTES_JOURNAL_2022_ENTRY_IN_TRANSIT

After I set up my tent next to the freeway near the Dublin BART station parking garage and laid down inside my tent for approximately twenty minutes, the voices became extremely irritating. They insinuated that I was being prevented from introspecting and, instead, forced to talk to them about what they wanted to talk about (which was more shallow and politically conservative than what I wanted to think about). I took down the tent and put it away and walked to a relatively quiet place near where new homes are being constructed. I had a vivid and lengthy dream about joining a restaurant crew and having to get a vaccination shot into my lip right there in the restaurant in front of the other crew members while I said in moderate shock, “Not my face!” (because I thought the shot would be delivered to my arm instead of my face). I walked around the restaurant and felt that I was just waiting to be told what to do by the restaurant managers and more seasoned new hires. The dream flashed to me being inside of a small and stark natural colored wood box of a residence which had a sink and some cabinets. I walked outside to some nearby cabin next to what looked like Lake Tahoe in the summer time (without the trees). An old Chinese man (i.e. the restaurant owner) was in there and seemed jovial and asked me if I was comfortable enough to switch into my red work shirt and black work shoes right there and I said yes and put the red shirt on instead of my bright yellow shirt and the nonslip close toed shoes instead of my brown shoes. Then I walked with him and several other employees out of that building and the dream flashed to that kitchenette I was apparently living in and the employees started stocking the shelves and countertops with individually plastic wrapped lemon cakes and other bakery items and put inventory sheets on the walls. The dream flash forward to me being able to run and jump tirelessly while an old friend from my childhood named Andrea was driving a giant seated scooter which I was supposed to be on the back of but was running late so she took off without me. She said, “Race me to the top,” and I jubilantly ran almost as fast as she drove up some dirt path on a mountain which lead to the restaurant on top. Then I jumped as high as I would if I were on the moon and went inside the restaurant to see that it was a store whose interior was similar to this kitchenette. We were told to get a hand cart and to fill that cart with items we thought we needed. I opened a cabinet and saw a large collection of anime DVDs. Apparently, Andrea was my roommate and fellow employee. Then I woke up around 9AM. As soon as I started thinking about working on my website today, that killjoy pissy female voice started talking incessantly. I am hiding in my sleeping bag from the bright hot sunlight which is making me sweat while typing this note into a Gmail draft using my phone’s cracked screen. I hear the sound of nearby construction. I’m not sure where I am going today.

* * *

Anyway, I feel significantly better than I did yesterday. Yes, I acknowledge that my mental health tends to fluctuate and can be a bit questionable, but as of late (especially while being immersed in a place which feels relatively impersonal, modern, and reminiscent of my early college years back when I had a sense of open-endedness and enthusiasm about my future instead of boxed into the town I grew up in as an overgrown leech with nowhere to hide). I am actually quite happy today. As I was walking through the streets on my way back to “The Bunny Bush” where I left my bags of cereal, I was walking through the Dublin Station apartment buildings and remembered how, back in August of 2015, Spencer and I almost got to live together in one of those apartments upstairs. I do not know what happened which caused me to miss out on that opportunity and choose to get scammed by Alan instead of sticking to Spencer. I miss Spencer. He was the best friend I ever had. I love how futuristic, intelligent, nerdy, and self-driven he was. I do not long for anyone else’s friendship. So it surprised me that I fell for Alan; a guy who was a lot more chauvinistic and mean; a guy who I knew did not want a serious relationship and just wanted to get rid of me and leave me feeling abandoned and stripped of my identity, past, and future while stranded in a desolate and cede place. Anyway, that reminds me of how I am starting to feel now (but in a good way): a lot more comfortable resigning myself to live in the present and to not dwell on thoughts about the past nor the future too much. After all, the past and the future are just noumena at this point. Only the present moment as I render it “in real time” is phenomenal to me. It is so much easier and pragmatic for me to focus my attention on what is relatively real rather than imaginary.

Anyway, as I walked towards the big clogged freeway full of car traffic, I thought about how I wanted to kill myself if I thought I could not have the future I wanted. It’s like me saying, “If I think I will not be able to finish this puzzle I am working on which means so much to me, then I will just kill myself so that I do not have to suffer that disappointment.” I could retort by saying, “You are never certain that the future will turn out any particular way. Also, there is a chance you will botch your suicide. You might as well stay alive and keep an open and uncertain mind about the future.”

Okay. That settles it. Onto the next subject…

Last night I was too tired and irritable to talk about this one, but I am ready now: how “love” is suddenly such a big trending topic (especially amongst new agers and social justice warriors and even political conservatives). When I was a child, I remember “love” being treated more like a religious topic than a political one. I remember the words of my elementary school’s anthem which all of the kids would sing together on Friday mornings in the courtyard:

Sing Stanton Song

That’s the place where we can all belong.

Computers, PE, our campout too. And people who get along.

Let’s sing. Stanton Song.

And a nice piano plays the instrumental part amongst innocent and hopeful sounding children’s voices. It makes me a bit misty eyed recalling it.

Anyway, that song is not about love. It’s about collaboration, learning, and living in the real world; not in living in a fucked up psychiatric ward where we have to have our eyes glazed over and creepy emotional intensity with a self righteous and quasi religious tone.

I am grossed out by how “love” has recently been touted as some kind of universal, unifying ethical value. It feels very anti scientific and forced to be religious and forced to have a certain kind of sentimentality I find forced, anthropocentric, and anti naturalistic…something which makes the real me feel iced out, gagged, and bound.

On Twitter, someone asked via a tweet what people would define as “love”. I answered: “pure nothingness”.

In the Christian Bible, God is said to be synonymous with love. If I use a pantheist definition of God (which fits well with my pansychic ideology), the answer I gave to that question is perfectly logical..but it is redundant. Why not just call it pure nothingness instead of make it into something so limited, favoritist, and irrational sounding? I am turned off by mushy fluffy language. It offends the hard nosed scientist in me.

Anyway, I am considering re-adding the page named PANPSYCHISM to my website just so I can elaborate on the possibility that the panpsychism hypothesis is not true. I am not sure if that is a good use of my resources.
