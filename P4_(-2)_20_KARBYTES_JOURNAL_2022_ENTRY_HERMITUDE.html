KARBYTES_JOURNAL_2022_ENTRY_HERMITUDE

As soon as I opened up Sublime Text editor to write this note, the dryer stopped working for some reason unknown to me. (Methinks the police might be able to control the electrical circuits of my house remotely. Perhaps they do not want me to get too comfortable living inside this house). Anyway, I came home to see that my mom and dad were both there watching the news in my dad’s room. They left some baked potatoes and corn on the cob for me (which I ate and was grateful for). I saw my cat Brea for a while but she seemed a bit hurt that I was gone for so long and kind of gave me the cold shoulder. Well, I feel bad about the mean things I said to that guy today. I wish I did not. I also feel like I should have done more to help him somehow. If I was not so selfish, I would have been more motivated to work very hard for pay and I would let homeless people live on my property. I know how impractical that sounds. If I had lots of money, I would single handedly end homelessness and feed everyone for free. Just kidding. Somehow, all those resources would have to be procured. They do not just come out of nowhere. What I really care about most (as a social issue) is saving the environment. It is the one thing I feel everyone else (well, everyone who drives a petroleum powered car) is entitled to put off worrying about except for me. I do not want to be a sellout and drive a petroleum powered car nor give up eating a 100% plant based diet. That is the one thing I do for society which makes me feel like I am mitigating my harmful impact on everyone else. That is what enables me to justify getting discounts on groceries and being able to take so much time off from working for pay. Most jobs seem to demand that I drive a car. If I drive a car just so that I can commute to work in a timely manner, then I feel that I am doing little to make the world a better place. In fact, if all I do is drive to my menial labor job just so that I can line my pockets with cash and pay for my living expenses, all I am really doing is taking care of my immediate wants and needs at the expense of the environment and helping other people feel more justified in doing the same thing (by insinuating through my example that they too do not have to take environmental issues seriously as I currently do).

So as easy as it is for me to settle back into living indoors if it were not for the irritating interference of the police voices talking to me through electromagnetic waves beamed to my skull, I believe that it is easier for me to think for myself and to live as a household of one if I stick to living out of a backpack and spending most of my time outdoors. It is quite a sacrifice for me to give up living in a house like most of the people around me get to do, but most of such people have small children to take care of. It would be unreasonable to expect such people to live like I do. An able bodied adult doing what I do is justifiable, but someone with small kids doing that seems a bit insane, impractical, and cruel (for the children to have to put up with). Also, children are high maintenance and require constant supervision and are not as prepared to handle the harsh realities of living outdoors as are adults who are seasoned at the “sport” (like I am). Sometimes I feel resentment over the fact that other people are rewarded merely for popping out babies and using the fact that they have dependent minors as a golden ticket to enjoy more luxuries and comforts than I get to enjoy, but what those people do not get to enjoy that I do is lots of time alone in the wilderness and getting to exercise, eat, and sleep as it suits me to do so rather than adhere to a more demanding schedule which does not allow for much free time. In other words, I get to be more self-focused than the average adult because I am practically living as a hermit who hardly talks to anyone and who hardly asks anything of anyone. In other words, I do my best to mitigate how much I rely on others and how much I inconvenience them. Still, I feel that it is not enough just to take care of myself and to burden other people as little as possible. I feel that I am not doing enough to be more than just a bum unless I use my intellect to contribute meaningful works of art to society and to educate and to help educate others about issues I think matter. Given the fact that I have so much free time, I should be using as much of that time as possible to be well educated. Being able to study for many hours a day while most people hardly have an hour to spare is a priveledge. It means that I have a moral obligation to contribute my unusually high intelligence to making the world a better place and not just hoarding that knowledge for my own private enjoyment. That is why I have made myself an open (source) book. That is why I have practically zero privacy while others are guaranteed a privacy bubble to retreat to when things get tiring and difficult. Other people are able to indulge in distractions and robust sheltered environments than I am.

As I write this note, that little girl voice is whining and hyperventilating again. Perhaps she wants me to feel bad and not even feel that I am doing anything to merit any kind of gratitude from other people. Perhaps she is a police representative paid to cater the egos of those who want me to be groomed to think that I am nothing but a narcissist and public burden who is not allowed to feel worthy of human decency. Instead, she seems to be speaking on behalf of people who want me to feel ostracized, condemned, and tortured because I failed to contribute to society in the ways they think matters and because I have “sinned” in ways they deem to be unforgivable or, at the very least, deserving of painful punishment for their sadistic amusement and to mitigate any jealousy they might feel about me appearing to have advantages they do not have. Remember: the environment is not a person (and in a people-oriented world, only people matter).

After my clothes finish drying, I am heading off to my camp site to continue using my laptop computer (hopefully in more peace than the family-centric neighborhood I am currently sitting in and feeling barely tolerated inside of). I do not mean to suggest that the people around me are being intolerant of my existence. What I mean to suggest is that the police who watch over this neighborhood do not want me to hang out here more than three hours per month.

(I am having difficulty publishing this note because someone keeps booting me off the Internet. For that reason (and for other reasons), I am strongly considering taking my hair care supplies and laundry detergent to my mom’s house so that I can use her house instead of my dad’s house for backup Internet, electronics charging, showering, and laundry. Rather than spend hours hiking into the deep woods to camp, perhaps I will get a better deal camping at the horse pasture on the west side of Castro Valley overlooking San Leandro instead of this place which feels like the deep south of the United States of America).
