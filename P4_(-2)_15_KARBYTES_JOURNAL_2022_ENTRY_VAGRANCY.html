KARBYTES_JOURNAL_2022_ENTRY_VAGRANCY

The last two journal entry pages which I added to this website seem a bit trashy compared to many other “pink bin” pages I have added to this website.

This journal entry is about how I am sick of breathing in more freeway fumes than necessary and spending too much time in places where garbage piles up and where there is lots of noise from human activity. I do not want to have to be deprived of the relatively clean air of the hilly wilderness regions near where my legal address is located in Castro Valley just because some people seem to think I do not deserve to enjoy such a relatively peaceful and relatively secluded environment in which to work on my website, study, meditate, and sleep.

I want to extend my lifespan and to maximize my quality of life as much as possible. Therefore, I should avoid spending time inhaling any more pollution than necessary, avoid getting too much ultraviolet light skin exposure, and avoid being in crowded urban areas and take advantage of the fact that I can hang out in more naturalistic environments which are less crowded with people and less filled with people’s trash, noise, and light pollution.

I deserve to get a proper white collar job instead of settling for working in menial labor or in customer service jobs. Perhaps I should just be a full time student until I can get a job doing something which utilizes my computer science background and information technology expertise. It costs me more to settle for a relatively unskilled job because such jobs tend to make me feel that I am wasting my time, energy, and skills.

If it takes me years to get a proper job, I think that is better than rushing to take a menial job which only demoralizes me and makes me feel that I am settling for being a capybara. I think it is about time that I seriously stand up for myself and be as white collar professional as I want to be. I deserve to make computer programming, science literacy, and engineering a core part of my life rather than hobbies I only get to dabble in once in a while and mostly in secret. If other people protest or try to sabotage me, oh well. I will just keep inventing creative ways to get by without having to lower my standard of living any more than necessary. This is the twenty first century. This is the Information Age; not the Industrial Age.

Then again, at the last minute, I might just take a part time or full time menial labor job doing food service, customer service, package handling, or something similar because I decide that is the path of least resistance. I am just getting these ideas out while those ideas are fresh in my mind. Perhaps I am better off doing some kind of “mindless” and “unskilled” job to subsidize or to complete cover the cost of my relatively intellectual hobbies.

Perhaps I will spend more time in the urban dirt where homeless people and heroin addicts seem to have inhabited. Perhaps I will have to leave the pristine parks and go back into the urban environments to advance my life. Perhaps I will be happiest just not worrying too much about the pollution. (After all, I could just walk farther away from the freeway to camp instead of settle down right next to the freeway).
