While walking through the dark to my camp sight in the woods, I was thinking about several things which pertain to why I dislike humanity in general. Firstly, as much as I sincerely desire to be my own best friend and significant other and to spend my life going on solitary adventures and having deep intimate intrapersonal (rather than interpersonal) conversations with myself about the nature of existence and such (and whatever else I find interesting and worthwhile), I cannot help but feel that other people are preventing me from enjoying that kind of richness of solitude. It seems as though, in order to get my most basic needs met in this society and to avoid being punished, I have to pretend that I am not the only person I sincerely want to hang out with. It seems that having human friends other than myself is implicitly mandatory according to most other humans. Unless I regularly hang out with other humans and enjoy their company, it seems that human society in general treats me like I am mentally ill at best and a villain at worst. Either way, I get the sense that other humans are not okay with me indulging my asocial desires. It seems that other humans are more indoctrinated than I am into believing that each human is required to contribute to society by taking interest in other humans because, if they do not, each human would have to fend for itself in isolation. I am not opposed to helping other humans if and when I can (and I am glad that trained human specialists exist whose jobs are to help other humans). I do think that humans could be more self sufficient and less dependent on each other if (a) humans were freer to pursue an education on their own terms and (b) if as much essential labor as possible is outsourced to robots so that humans could live for free on a universal basic income and only work as volunteers rather than as slaves. When humans are forced to work, they seem embittered. I would like to see contributing to society become optional rather than mandatory so that only the humans who have that kind of altruistic desire do that kind of work. It would help me see how many humans really do care about beings other than themselves rather than just pretending that they do (and if only 5% of the human population ends up significantly contributing to serving more than just themselves, it would be nice to let the other 95% of the human population just harmlessly rest on their laurels and leech from the system which pampers the babies they are rather than force those brats to do jobs which motivated and qualified humans and robots would do more effectively and efficiently). Secondly, I am grossed out that a lot of spiritual teachers insinuate that femininity is the equal and complementary opposite to masculinity when I cannot help but believe that femininity as humans express it is mostly in service to masculinity as humans express it and never the other way around. Otherwise, human bodies and minds would be much more androgynous and less sexually dimorphic. Look at how human females have been selectively bred to have only the traits which appeal to the male humans. Male humans used their superior physical strength to coerce female humans into being what the male humans wanted them to be such that, over many generations, such a power discrepancy became even more exagerated. I cannot see femininity as equal to masculinity for that reason. I see femininity as inferiority in a masculinity-oriented species.
