While walking through the dark to my camp sight in the woods, I was thinking about several things which pertain to why I dislike humanity in general. Firstly, as much as I sincerely desire to be my own best friend and significant other and to spend my life going on solitary adventures and having deep intimate intrapersonal (rather than interpersonal) conversations with myself about the nature of existence and such (and whatever else I find interesting and worthwhile), I cannot help but feel that other people are preventing me from enjoying that kind of richness of solitude. It seems as though, in order to get my most basic needs met in this society and to avoid being punished, I have to pretend that I am not the only person I sincerely want to hang out with. It seems that having human friends other than myself is implicitly mandatory according to most other humans. Unless I regularly hang out with other humans and enjoy their company, it seems that human society in general treats me like I am mentally ill at best and a villain at worst. Either way, I get the sense that other humans are not okay with me indulging my asocial desires. It seems that other humans are more indoctrinated than I am into believing that each human is required to contribute to society by taking interest in other humans because, if they do not, each human would have to fend for itself in isolation. I am not opposed to helping other humans if and when I can (and I am glad that trained human specialists exist whose jobs are to help other humans). I do think that humans could be more self sufficient and less dependent on each other if (a) humans were freer to pursue an education on their own terms and (b) if as much essential labor as possible is outsourced to robots so that humans could live for free on a universal basic income and only work as volunteers rather than as slaves. When humans are forced to work, they seem embittered. I would like to see contributing to society become optional rather than mandatory so that only the humans who have that kind of altruistic desire do that kind of work. It would help me see how many humans really do care about beings other than themselves rather than just pretending that they do (and if only 5% of the human population ends up significantly contributing to serving more than just themselves, it would be nice to let the other 95% of the human population just harmlessly rest on their laurels and leech from the system which pampers the babies they are rather than force those brats to do jobs which motivated and qualified humans and robots would do more effectively and efficiently). Secondly, I am grossed out that a lot of spiritual teachers insinuate that femininity is the equal and complementary opposite to masculinity when I cannot help but believe that femininity as humans express it is mostly in service to masculinity as humans express it and never the other way around. Otherwise, human bodies and minds would be much more androgynous and less sexually dimorphic. Look at how human females have been selectively bred to have only the traits which appeal to the male humans. Male humans used their superior physical strength to coerce female humans into being what the male humans wanted them to be such that, over many generations, such a power discrepancy became even more exagerated. I cannot see femininity as equal to masculinity for that reason. I see femininity as inferiority in a masculinity-oriented species. I personally feel blessed as a female bodied human to have a naturally androgynous build and to have had the opportunity to inject myself with testosterone every two weeks for a period of 1.5 years from 2009 to 2010. I decided to stop the injections after I got the permanently deepened voice I wanted and some bone structure and hair growth changes I wanted because (a) my boyfriend at the time protested my hormone use and (b) I was afraid that I was going to run out of money and access to those hormones and did not want to be so dependent on something outside of myself for well-being. In hindsight, I kind of regret quitting the hormones. I might have been happier if I continued because, by now, I would have a beard and be phenotypically identical to a man except for the genitals and chromosomes. Oh well. At this stage in my life, I am trying to not be so shallow and just accept myself being a somewhat masculine-androgynous woman. I decided that it is easiest to just let people call me whatever pronouns and slurs they want instead of beg them to treat me how I want them to (though I will try my best to error on the side of resembling a man than a woman because I see almost no advantage to being percieved as a woman instead of as a man). The only "point" of being a woman, in my opinion, is to be a prostitute and get heterosexual men to give her material advantages and emotional validation in exchange for gratifying his sexual desires and carrying out the seemingly obligatory task of birthing the next generation of humans. (I would not mind if humans went extinct because not enough human females sincerely want to have babies. I also hope that human reproduction is outsourced to artificial wombs and that cloning humans from computer optimized human genomes and stem cells effectively renders traditional human sexual reproduction obsolete. If that happens, sexual dimorphism in humans would exist solely for aesthetic and recreational purposes). There! Now that I got the relatively petty shit out of the way, I can start to address the deeper stuff I really want to talk about... As I was setting up my tent, I was thinking about how disatisfied I feel with art in general. Seemingly every deliberate human endeavor exists for purely egotistical reasons. "Look at me! Look at me! Aren't I awesome?! Aren't you impressed with how talented I am?! Don't you appreciate how much investment went into what I brought to fruition with mine own hands?!" There is nothing I see inherently wrong with being an egotist. What I do not like is having to perform for an audience other than myself (according to other people). Otherwise, what I do does not deserve to be appreciated even by myself (according to those other people). I hate those other people. What I am trying to promote is extreme solipsism. It is a beautiful ideology; the idea that I can be the only target audience my artwork needs and that I do not have to passive aggressively bait other people into noticing and appreciating what I do like some kind of needy whore.
