KARBYTES_JOURNAL_2022_ENTRY_GETTING_SANER

Rather than go back to the place I was camping at during the last three days or so near Lake Chabot in Castro Valley, I decided to set up my tent here in Dublin next to the BART station because I think doing so will save me time and make it easier to get my website up to date as quickly as possible. I cannot yet tell which voices are worse for me: the ones at my camping spot in Castro Valley or the ones at my camping spot in Dublin. They are both quite annoying and demoralizing. I prefer the clean air, relative quietude, and natural environment of my Castro Valley camping spot, but my Dublin camping spot is located right next to several public electrical wall outlets, gas stations with bathrooms that are open 24 hours a day, and the Dublin region feels more open, modern, and not quite as claustrophobic (except for the voices which might be more smothering to me than the voices which harass me in Castro Valley).

The last few times I have been camping in Castro Valley, the voices would insinuate that I have to think like a teenager no older than 16 years old and focus on my parents and ex boyfriend. On the Dublin side of the hills, I seem to be treated like I am at least 21 years old and the atmosphere over here feels like being in college because there are pubs, restaurants, shopping centers, sleek office buildings, and modern upscale apartments nearby. This side feels less isolated while not feeling excessively familial. What I mean to suggest is that I feel that I can more easily hide in the background in Dublin and just hunker down and do my projects without it being a huge spectacle to neighors in a small town where many people seem to know me.

Anyway, my mental health seems to be improving. I am slightly happier than I was a few days ago. Today I updated Karlina Object dot WordPress dot Com and finalized the NATURE and MULTIVERSE pages. If I stay up all night tonight, I might be able to get the first eight pages finalized. Then, after that, I can publish those finalized page links to my social media feeds and start adding more pages to the website. (It is approximately 8:45PM right now. The sun set yet the sky is still fairly bright and mostly cloudless. In the distance towards the north I heard the trumpet song playing from the army base. That always cheers me up for some reason. In some ways, I feel similar to an army camp person even though I consider myself to be an organization of one who is not affiliated with any other organization).

I am warming up to the idea of eventually getting back into working in the food service industry part time while claiming to be a college student. It would be nice to work in a place which hires college students and rather than high school students. I think I would enjoy the job much more if I knew I was not the only college-level employee (and I only want to work in places which cater to vegans and people with sophisticated preferences rather than settle for something cheap and ghetto like junky fast food franchises (and I prefer to work in gentrified areas rather than impoverished, trash strewn slums)).

Anyway, I am a bit embarrassed for all the complaining I did about sexual dimorphism and hyper sexualized culture in humans. I think my mind was in the gutter for to long. I felt alienated from what feels like my cultural home base. Getting two of the most important pages of my website up to date helped me feel more at home in the world. Now I have a little house on the Internet where my authentic soul can live virtually forever. It is a warm, glowing cabin waiting for me whenever I need it (and other people are invited to glean comfort and inspiration from that little cyberspace refuge as well).

It helps to surf the web and look at random entertaining or thought provoking content online when I need a break from being in pensive workaholic mode. I know my way around the Internet fairly well now. I know several websites which usually have news I find worth my time to consume. When I really want a mental vacation, I walk over to the Whole Foods Market Wi-Fi to download long videos to watch in my tent.

Finally, I appreciate the kindness of the police who sometimes drive by to ask me if I need any homelessness resources as part of a welfare checkup. I feel cared about and safe here. I know it might be disconcerting to see that I am still living outside after all these years, but I have never been happier. This might be the closest I ever get to being able to move out of my parents’ household. I am afraid I might not ever be able to afford to rent an apartment even if I work full time. Hence, my plan is to make it look like I live with my parents or share living quarters with other renters so that people do not think I am a grungy, insane homeless person while living in a tent and only going home no more than once a week to shower and do laundry. (It is hard to hide my lifestyle if I always have my big backpack (which has my tent strapped to it) with me, but I I feel most secure having it with me at all times. I’ll just tell people I am a bit obsessive compulsive about being ready to go into survival mode in case there is some zombie apocalypse or something).
